Fashion is changing in a major way. As sustainability becomes more important, people are turning to secondhand shopping, vintage styles, and digital platforms to express themselves without adding to fast fashion waste. Trends like TikTok thrift hauls, Depop reselling, and the return of early 2000s clothing have created a new culture where reuse and creativity feel both stylish and responsible. This website visualizes how these shifts are happening and how digital spaces are making sustainable fashion more mainstream.

This chart compares Google search interest for the terms “thrift,” “vintage clothing,” and “Poshmark” from 2010 to 2024. Searches for “thrift” rise steadily after 2018, which aligns with the period when Gen Z became more active online and began popularizing thrift hauls, Y2K aesthetics, and secondhand styling on platforms like TikTok and Instagram. Interest in Poshmark also increases sharply during this time, matching the growth of resale apps that are especially popular with younger users. While searches for “vintage clothing” were higher in earlier years, they gradually decrease as newer terms and app-based resale trends become more common. Together, these patterns reflect a cultural shift toward sustainable fashion practices, supported by the online habits and style preferences of younger audiences.

Why
Sustainable
Fashion Is
Rising Among
Gen Z?

Gen Z has played a major role in making sustainable fashion more visible. They use social media platforms to share thrift finds, promote secondhand shopping, and celebrate restyled outfits. Online communities on TikTok, Instagram, and Depop make it easy to discover trends, buy and sell items, and participate in fashion culture without relying on fast fashion. This digital influence encourages people to reuse clothing and explore creative ways to express themselves. The rise of secondhand shopping and online resale reflects how Gen Z is redefining modern fashion through sustainability and self expression.
